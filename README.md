## One-page executive summary

### Is it a valid business idea?

**Conditional YES** — but not as a "three businesses in one" concept. The panel reframes it as:

> A **land-appreciation play** on a specific highway-adjacent Pondicherry plot, carried by three operating businesses that cover holding cost while the land compounds.

The businesses are the rent on the land, not the investment thesis. If you cannot find a plot that (a) has genuine locational scarcity, (b) permits physical segregation of truck and car forecourts, and (c) has residual value as a godown / petrol-pump site if all three businesses fail — walk away and index the capital.

See [01-panel.md](01-panel.md) for the full 9-expert panel (Christensen, Porter, Drucker, Godin, Kim & Mauborgne, Collins, Taleb, Meadows, Doumont) in sequential-then-debate format. Final panel verdict: **CONDITIONAL GO** with 5 kill-criteria.

### Weighbridge: 40-ft or 60-ft?

**60-ft (18 m), 80-ton, pitless.** Not 40-ft.

- 40-ft platform (12 m) only fits 2-axle rigids and 14-wheelers. Misses the tractor-semitrailer / 22-wheeler multi-axle class, which dominates granite, container, and cement traffic on the Cuddalore–Chennai corridor.
- Cost delta is only ~₹5 L. Revenue delta is ~30–40% of addressable market, permanently.
- Go 100-ton only if field diligence shows regular overloaded trucks.

Full derivation in [02-sizing-loop.md](02-sizing-loop.md).

### How much land?

| Case | Land | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Low | 0.5 acre | Only if corner/T-junction with natural dual access |
| **Expected (recommended)** | **0.65–0.75 acre** | **Clears segregation + 60-ft weighbridge + 6 EV bays + 1,750 sqft coffee shop** |
| High | 1.0 acre | Future-proofs 2030 EV demand + better residual-use exit |

Minimum highway frontage: **~65 m** for dual HV/LV access. See [04-site-plan.md](04-site-plan.md) for zone-by-zone breakdown and ASCII site diagram.

### How many EV chargers?

**6 bays at go-live (4 DC fast + 2 AC slow), pad prepped for 8.** Partner with a named CPO (Tata Power / Statiq / ChargeZone / Ather) under revenue-share — you are the landlord, not the operator. Do not proceed without a CPO site LOI in hand.

Footprint: ~260 sqm / 2,800 sqft. See [03-ev-and-coffee.md](03-ev-and-coffee.md) Part A.

### How much space for the coffee shop?

**~1,750 sqft (165 sqm) building** + shared ~1,400 sqft parking.

Two-counter design: premium side for cars/EVs, trucker side for HV drivers. **Twin restroom blocks** — segregated from each other. Restrooms are the single biggest differentiator on Indian highways.

See [03-ev-and-coffee.md](03-ev-and-coffee.md) Part B.

### Total envelope (expected case)

- **Land:** ₹75 L–₹1.0 Cr (0.75 acre at Bahour-outskirts commercial rates — verify for your plot)
- **Build + equipment + reserves:** ~₹1.3 Cr
- **Total: ~₹2.0–2.3 Cr**

See [04-site-plan.md](04-site-plan.md) capex table.

## The 5 diligence questions you must answer before buying land

From the panel synthesis. Detail in [06-diligence-checklist.md](06-diligence-checklist.md).

1. **Verified truck count** at the specific plot — 4-week manual count, not corridor averages.
2. **Competitor weighbridge utilization** within 8 km — visit each, pay, time it.
3. **NHAI 2030 master plan** check for bypasses or re-alignments within 5 km of the plot.
4. **Named CPO site LOI** before any land advance — no LOI, no EV thesis.
5. **Residual land-use appraisal** — if all three businesses fail, does the land still stand on its own?
